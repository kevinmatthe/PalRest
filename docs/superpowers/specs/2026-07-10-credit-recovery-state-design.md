# Credit Recovery State Design

## Goal

Prevent credit from recovering while a player is online and expose the amount actually credited during the player's most recently completed offline interval.

## Recovery Semantics

Credit recovers only while a player is offline. A recovery interval is settled when an offline player is observed online again. Continuous online observations never recover credit, and querying an online player's snapshot never changes or temporarily inflates available credit.

The most recent recovery amount means the actual credit added during the last completed offline-to-online transition. It is updated only when such a transition is settled. A player who is currently offline retains the value from the previous completed transition; uncommitted live offline recovery is not reported as the most recent recovery.

Recovery is capped by `credit_max`. The recorded amount is the actual increase after applying the cap, not the theoretical amount generated by elapsed offline time. A completed interval that produces no increase records zero.

## Persistence

Add `last_credit_recovered_ms` to `policy_states` with a non-negative default of zero. It belongs to the existing `(user_id, policy_revision)` state, so changing the effective Policy revision or resetting player Policy state naturally clears the displayed recovery history.

The credit accrual helper returns both the updated state and actual recovered duration. The caller persists `last_credit_recovered_ms` only when settling an offline-to-online transition. Ordinary online consumption updates available credit and timestamps without replacing the most recent recovery amount.

Existing databases migrate in place and retain all current credit balances. Old rows report no recovery record until the next completed offline interval.

## Observation and Snapshot Flow

During `Observe` the service determines continuity before refreshing strategy state:

- Continuous online player: do not accrue credit; consume the observed online interval.
- First observation after absence: accrue from `last_credit_at` to the current observation, persist the actual recovered amount, and establish new online continuity without charging the absent interval.
- First-ever state: initialize at maximum credit and report no prior recovery.

`Snapshot` may calculate a non-persisted projected available credit only for an offline player. For an online player it reads the persisted current credit without accrual. The most recent completed recovery remains the persisted value in both cases.

## API and WebUI

For credit-strategy players, player responses add:

- `credit_available_ms`: currently available credit. For an offline player this may include the read-only projected recovery as of the request time.
- `last_credit_recovered_ms`: actual credit added on the last completed offline-to-online transition.

The fields are omitted for other strategies. The existing `remaining_ms` remains compatible and equals available credit for the credit strategy.

The player usage cell displays the current available credit and a secondary `Last recovery` value. When `last_credit_recovered_ms` is zero, it displays `No recovery recorded`.

## Testing

Tests cover:

- Continuous online observations consume credit without recovery.
- Online snapshot queries do not recover credit.
- Offline-to-online transition settles recovery exactly once.
- Recovery records the actual capped increase.
- Current offline projection does not overwrite the last completed recovery.
- SQLite migration and state round trip preserve the new field.
- API fields are present only for credit players.
- WebUI renders available and last-recovery values, including the empty state.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Credit never increases during continuous online play or online snapshot reads.
2. Returning from offline settles the correct recovery once and persists the actual capped amount.
3. The API and WebUI show current available credit and the most recently completed recovery amount.
4. Existing databases migrate without losing balances or Policy state.
5. All Go, frontend, race, build, and static verification commands pass before merge.
